What are uilleann (il-an) pipes?

Uilleann Pipes are Ireland's national bagpipes, as opposed to the far more common and well known Scottish Highland Bagpipes. Uilleann pipes are characterized by a quieter and mellower timbre, as well as a more versatile playing range. Instead of standing, an uilleann piper plays sitting down, the pipes resting on the lap. They are best known for their appearances in movies like Braveheart and Titanic. More about the uilleann pipes can be read here.

As uilleann pipes are an Irish instrument, they are not played in Scottish dress (kilts).

They are great for any kind of event, including weddings, funerals, teas, clan gatherings, anniversaries, and parties.
